Advice I received that should be passed on to other entry-level Devlopers

In my first couple of weeks as a Software Engineering intern, I was talking to a colleague who was a few years my senior. I went over to ask him a question about the error I was seeing in the console. And, I'm not exaggerating when I share with you that, as soon as I set my Mac on his desk, I realized what I needed to do to relieve the error.

He laughed and said, "It happens to me all the time" and shared that he always asks any questions he has to his Penguin first. I looked at him a little cock-eye, and he pointed to the 2" X 1" foam penguin sitting on the back left corner of his desk. He said,

"Nine times out of ten, I know the answer, and it might just take me asking someone or taking a quick break to figure it out on my own, so I ask my Penguin first".

I don't have a Penguin on my desk. Instead I use a mug from my time working in London as my "Penguin." And, it really works. It's a silly fun habit, but it really makes you a better self-solver and co-worker; not to mention a bit cheerier. ;) This is probably the most memorable advice I received within the first six months of software engineering, and I hope it can be helpful, and fun for you if you choose to adopt it as well.
